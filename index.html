Abstract

Recently, the field of artificial intelligence has witnessed remarkable progress in the development of text-to-image diffusion models. These models have demonstrated a significant capability in generating realistic and diverse images. Notably, some of these models have been shown to excel in producing images that exhibit a high degree of numeracy and spatial reasoning, especially when provided with complex prompts. However, despite these advancements, a persistent challenge remains in the form of severe object overlapping issues. This problem is particularly critical for various real-world applications, such as in the design of floor plans. In response to this, we present NOPS, aimed at mitigating object overlapping issues through comprehensive prompt engineering. Furthermore, this study has initiated the development of novel and more complex benchmarks, along with an evaluation metric specifically designed to assess overlapping and object layout accuracy. This approach represents a significant stride towards enhancing the practical utility of text-to-image diffusion models in real-world scenarios.
